CNC Sand Coffee Table

Inspired by the Sisyphus Sand Table and the ZenXY, this sand table was created through an old glass table top and spare wood.  3D printing software, Klipper, was flashed to an Arduino Uno, and is abled to be controlled through a Raspberry Pi.  Images in the sand could be generated through sandify.org, which users can hand create various line works, similar to vector drawing programs.  To control the LEDs in the table, WLED is used for network control and Home Assistant integration.

In order to match the curved glasstop, the corners were measured and 3D printed to get the exact curve.  The wood covering below the glass was lasercut to exact size.  The legs and sidings were all hand cut with a jigsaw.  Afterwards it was painted accordingly.

The sand tabled pictured is revision two.  The first revision used linear rails and a sub optimal belt path which caused it to misalign itself after drawing pictures in the sand.

Laser Engraved LED Picture Frame

Originally starting as simple laser engraved wooden posters, it was evolved to become a picture frame.  The original artwork is hand traced in Inkscape in order to get a vectorized image of the poster.  That image is laser engraved onto an 11" x 11" piece of wood.  The image is then mirrored and laser engraved again onto an 11" x 11" piece of acrylic.  The acrylic is mirrored so when it is turned around and placed ontop of the wood, you cannot feel the engraved lines.

The width of the wood and acrylic was measured and a frame was 3D modeled in Fusion 360.  The frame is printed in four pieces along with four corners, all printed upright to avoid overhangs.  An LED strip is strung through the frame and dupont connectors are crimped to the LED strip in order for it to be easily rearranged for a college dorm room.  A custom PCB was CNC'd out of a single layer copper FR4 board.  This was done to make wiring neater and as a proof of concept.

With the LEDs off, the wood engraving is shown.  But with the LEDs on, the acrylic engraving is lit, and shows off the detailed linework that was engraved.

Student Engineering Council Funding Application Spring 2022

After noticing a deficit in the 3D printing in our local makerspace, a proposal was written in order to get funding for improvement.  A 15 page document outlined the need for new 3D printers and how to optimize the queue to be faster.  After presenting this document to the department heads, I presented on behalf of Virginia Tech's CEED to receive funding for better equipment in our makerspace.  The Student Engineering Council accepted our proposal and CEED was granted $3600.

3D Printed Iron Man Helmet

Using four servos and an Arduino Nano, this 3D printed Iron Man helmet is able to move up and down while also turning the lights on and off.

The LED eyes are cut to size and were ordered from Aliexpress as cosplay eyes.  They can be dimmed with a simple potentiometer.  The Arduino Nano was powered off of 3 AA batteries and with a press of the button located on the helmet's ear, allowed the user to open and close the front.

The program controlling everything was written in Arudino C.
